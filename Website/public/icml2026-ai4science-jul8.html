AI for Science — workshops worth attending

The AI-for-Science-relevant workshops across both ICML workshop days (Jul 10–11). All run 08:00–17:00 KST. Picks flagged; the flagship AI for Science workshop (Jul 11, Hall C) has its full program at the bottom.

Friday, July 10

Day 1 leans biology + generative methods for science.

Jul 10·Hall D2·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
Generative and Agentic AI for Biology
Biomolecule and therapeutic design, hypothesis generation, and closed-loop wet-lab integration — the strongest pure-science workshop on Day 1.
Jul 10·Hall D1·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
4th Structured Probabilistic Inference & Generative Modeling (SPIGM)
Generative and probabilistic methods aimed squarely at molecules, physics, and scientific data — high overlap with the AI-for-Science poster themes.
Jul 10·Auditorium·08:00–17:00
AI as a Tool for Mathematics, Computer Science, and ML
Theorem proving, autoformalization, and math reasoning agents — the math-science thread of Day 1.
Jul 10·Room 308·08:00–17:00
Graph Foundation Models
Graph FMs underpin much of molecular / materials modeling; useful if you work on crystals, reactions, or PDE meshes.
Jul 10·Room 318·08:00–17:00
Foundations of Deep Generative Models
Method-side companion to SPIGM — diffusion / flow matching foundations that power the science generators.

Saturday, July 11

Day 2 is the science-heavy day: the flagship AI-for-Science workshop plus physics, math and life-sciences tracks all run in parallel — plan around room changes.

Jul 11·Hall C·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
AI for Science: AI Scientists — Tools, Co-authors, or Founders?
THE AI-for-Science workshop. Invited talks across chemistry, math, sampling and general science; two contributed-talk blocks with the best papers; and a benchmarking panel. Full program below.
Jul 11·Room 402·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
AI4Physics: AI for Physics
High-energy physics, astrophysics, condensed matter, plasma and fusion — the physics counterpart to the biology day.
Jul 11·Hall D1·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
3rd AI for Math Workshop: Toward Self-Evolving Scientific Agents
Self-evolving scientific agents, theorem proving, and scientific problem solving.
Jul 11·Room 317·08:00–17:00AI4Science pick
3rd Multi-modal Foundation Models & LLMs for Life Sciences (FM4LS)
Protein sequences and structures, genomics/transcriptomics, drug discovery and precision medicine.
Jul 11·Hall D2·08:00–17:00
Structured Data for Health
ML on structured clinical / biomedical data — adjacent to the life-sciences track.
Jul 11·Grand Ballroom 104-105·08:00–17:00
Forecasting as a New Frontier of Intelligence
Weather, climate and time-series forecasting — same territory as the Day-2/3 atmospheric-modeling posters.
